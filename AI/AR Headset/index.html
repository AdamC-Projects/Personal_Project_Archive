About: AR Headset

Why I made this one is simple:
I wanted an AR headset because I think they're really cool, but they were all too expensive, so I decided to make my own!

I coded the camera in January 2021, but held off on the project until after my GCSEs, when I then coded the UI and hand detection AI in July and early August.
Obviously, mine is at least half as good and twice as big as those on the market, so roughly 8x as inconvenient, but its still really cool :D

This project was my first proper project using the raspberryPI, my first actually useful implementation of a neural net I made in pyTorch and my first experience of actual frustration while coding... but it was worth it!
My frustration came from my camera cable being too short; there being no documentation I could find on the internet about how to make the mmalobj outputs render only semi-opaque; limited mmalobj documentation in general; only old versions of pytorch working for me on the rPI4; not being able to easily transfer neural networks to older versions of pytorch; my pi crashing every time I ran a version of my Headset with a higher resolution; and most importantly of all: having to write code onto my rPI from a tiny, awkward keyboard!!! (v n v)
Oh, and also the fact that my rPI4 completely broke just as I was about to finish - my files were luckily on a USB drive, but I had to reinstall raspbian, python all the python libraries required.

So, if you wanted to make something like this yourself, you've come to the right place! Let me be your beacon of hope ( ＾ᴗ＾ ) /

Download: AR Headset

Hardware:
Here are the products I purchased to make the AR headset:
• Raspberry Pi 4 Model B
• Raspberry Pi 4 Dark Case
• 5 inch 3D VR Headset
• PI 4 Wide Angle Infra Red Camera
• 5 inch HDMI Capacitive Touch Screen Monitor

Note: I cut a hole in the Pi case so the camera cable could run into the case; I cut a hole in the front of the headset to make room for the camera (which I screwed into position); I cut holes in the top so I could plug the power and HDMI cables into the screen, and holes in the top to allow the screen to fit. I also attached the Pi to the headset using velcro.
Be careful when doing all of this! You don't want to break anything!

Software:
This is the wheel I used to download pyTorch: torch-1.0.0a0+8322165-cp37-cp37m-linux_armv7l.whl
In order to screenshot the output from the camera (the best (in my experience) way to capture photos from the camera while it is streaming), I needed to install raspi2png.
