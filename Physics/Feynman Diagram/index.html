Feynman Diagram




Inspired by ScienceClic's video on feynman diagrams, I decided to write my own program that would randomly generate them.
I guess I was inspired by their simplicity in a mathematically complex field of physics - they gave me meaningful insight about virutal particles, and how our universe operates on a fundamental level.

Although my program is not mathematically accurate, I have followed the rules to generate a feynman diagram: I ensured my program obeyed the conservation of charge and momentum (or at least the conservation of momentum is close enough, I found if I allowed lines with steep graidents, it made the diagrams look messy [see code line 124]).
The x-axis represents a temporal dimension, the y-axis a spatial dimension.
